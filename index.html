About

Ben Link is a versatile trombonist, composer, educator, and graphic artist based in Chicago, Illinois. Ben honed his craft studying trombone performance at Indiana University under renowned professors David Baker and Dee Stewart, as well as at McNally Smith College of Music in St. Paul, MN with Peter Whitman and Scott Agster.

After college, Ben spent five years as a full-time member of the critically acclaimed touring band Davina and the Vagabonds, often traveling more than 250 days a year. During his tenure with the band, he had the honor of performing on the Jools Holland Show and at prestigious events like the Monterey Jazz Festival, among countless other venues across the country and abroad.

In addition to leading his own quartet and participating in a range of jazz and creative projects, Ben has played with a diverse array of ensembles across multiple genres. As an educator, Ben teaches private lessons and has led clinics in both Minnesota and Norway. As a graphic artist, Ben has designed cover art for albums and sold original digital and hand-drawn art.

Career Highlights

  • 18 years with ACME Jazz Co. big band
  • 10 years with Latin jazz orchestra Malamanya
  • 5 years touring with Davina and the Vagabonds (including Jools Holland Show and Monterey Jazz Festival)
  • 3 years playing every week with Minnesota blues legend James Samuel "Cornbread" Harris Jr. band
  • Educator at Minnesota Institute for Talented Youth (MITY)
  • Clinician in Minnesota and Norway
  • Performed with many more small groups, salsa groups and big bands, musicals, and creative projects
